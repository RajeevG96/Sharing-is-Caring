This is metadata (data which helps your content e.g. texts reach their intended recipient). This metadata is generated when you use internet or network based services. Companies and governments collect this metadata and analyse it. After analysis metadata can reveal a lot about your life including, your location at all times, your interests, political views, behaviours and friend groups. Organisations which gather this information are under no legal obligation to protect this data therefore this information about could end up literally anywhere.

"Even if you're not doing anything wrong, you're being watched and recorded" - Edward Snowden

In June 2013 Edward Snowden exposed how governments intentionally spy on their citizens.

He did this because whilst working for the NSA he found out that alongside other government intelligence agencies like GCHQ (UK) and the NSA (USA) were collecting massive amounts of digital data from ordinary internet users from all over the world i.e metadata via their internet connected devices and free online services.

How?

Intelligence agencies such as GCHQ and NSA have sophisticated hacking technology which is used to track and analyse YOU.

Technology used by the authorities to gather your data:

XKeyscore

Software used by intelligence agencies to search and analyse global internet traffic daily.

WARRIOR PRIDE

Software which can turn on, listen to and locate iPhone and Android mobile devices

Data Centers

The NSA have databanks of collected data including internet activity, phone call recordings and emails

Glenn Greenwald

(Lawyer/Journalist who reported on classified documents from the NSA released by Snowden regarding surveillance)

"NSA analysts can, via systems like XKeyscore, listen to whatever emails they want, whatever telephone calls, browsing histories, Microsoft Word documents. And it's all done with no need to go to a court, with no need to even get supervisor approval on the part of the analyst".

Not only is this the case for US citizens but it is true for almost all tech/internet users, including yourself.

Intelligence agencies like NSA in the USA and GCHQ in the UK can watch all of your digital communications without needing a warrant.
